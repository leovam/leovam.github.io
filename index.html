About me

I am currently a second-year Ph.D. student in Computational Biology at Carnegie Mellon University. I got my B.S. degree in Biological Science from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2012. I spent three years working as a research scholar in University of Michigan, investigated the neuron mechanism in Caenorhabditis elegans. Then I started my research in Computational Biology.

Research

My current project is to design a better algorithm to restore accurate copy number variations (CNVs) of genes in single cell data from bulk tumor data.

My interests lie in

  • Bulk Tumor Data Matrix Decompostion

  • Tumor Feature Dimension Reduction

  • Tumor Phylogenetic Tree Construction
